**The *Era of Bajirao* by Uday S. Kulkarni** is a deeply researched, richly illustrated historical account of Bajirao I’s rise and the expansion of Maratha power in the early 18th century. The book presents Bajirao’s twenty‑year leadership as a transformative period that reshaped the political map of India.

---

### 📘 Core Focus of the Book
- **Bajirao I as a historical phenomenon**  
  Kulkarni emphasizes how Bajirao, starting from a modest dominion of just two districts, built an empire that stretched across India within two decades. This period is framed as the definitive *Era of Bajirao*.

- **The Empire of the Deccan**  
  The narrative covers the Maratha expansion across the Deccan and beyond, detailing military campaigns, diplomacy, and administrative evolution.

---

### 📚 Structure and Features
The book is comprehensive and designed for serious historical study:

- **36 chapters** divided into **five sections**  
- **432–434 pages**, including:
  - 392+ pages of main text  
  - 28 preliminary pages  
  - 12 art pages  
- **27 pictures and 22 maps** integrated into the narrative  
- **Timelines, genealogies, principal character introductions**  
- **Appendices, references, bibliography, glossary, and index**  

These elements make the book both visually rich and academically robust.

---

### 🧭 Historical Scope
The coverage begins in the **late 17th century** and extends to the **mid‑18th century**, contextualizing Bajirao’s era within broader Maratha and Indian political developments.

---

### 👤 About the Author
Dr. Uday S. Kulkarni is a **surgeon turned historian** based in Pune. His long-standing interest in Maratha history and extensive field research—especially on the Third Battle of Panipat—inform the depth of his writing.

---

### 🧩 Why the Book Matters
- It offers **“history as it happened”**, supported by maps, primary references, and detailed annotations.  
- It is one of the most **comprehensive modern works** on Bajirao I, bridging academic rigor with accessible narrative.  
- The book helps readers understand how Bajirao’s leadership shaped the **political, military, and cultural landscape** of 18th‑century India.
