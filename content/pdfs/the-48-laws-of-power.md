@Overview

### **1–10: Mastering Perception and Influence**
1. **Never outshine the master** — Make superiors feel secure.  
2. **Never put too much trust in friends; learn to use enemies** — Friends can betray emotionally; enemies are predictable.  
3. **Conceal your intentions** — Keep plans hidden to avoid interference.  
4. **Always say less than necessary** — Power grows in silence and ambiguity.  
5. **Protect your reputation** — Reputation is the foundation of influence.  
6. **Court attention at all costs** — Visibility creates power.  
7. **Get others to do the work; take the credit** — Leverage others’ efforts.  
8. **Make people come to you** — Whoever controls the terms controls the power.  
9. **Win through actions, not argument** — Arguments breed resentment.  
10. **Avoid the unhappy and unlucky** — Emotions and misfortune are contagious.

### **11–20: Control, Strategy, and Leverage**
11. **Learn to keep people dependent on you** — Dependency equals control.  
12. **Use selective honesty to disarm** — A small truth can hide a larger deception.  
13. **Ask for help by appealing to self-interest** — People act for themselves.  
14. **Pose as a friend, work as a spy** — Information is power.  
15. **Crush your enemy totally** — Half-measures allow revenge.  
16. **Use absence to increase respect** — Scarcity enhances value.  
17. **Cultivate an air of unpredictability** — Keeps others off balance.  
18. **Do not build fortresses** — Isolation weakens awareness.  
19. **Know who you’re dealing with** — Misreading people is dangerous.  
20. **Do not commit to anyone** — Stay independent to stay powerful.


### **21–30: Psychological Advantage**
21. **Play the sucker to catch a sucker** — Appear weaker than you are.  
22. **Use surrender as a tool** — Yield temporarily to gain long-term advantage.  
23. **Concentrate your forces** — Focus energy on a single goal.  
24. **Play the perfect courtier** — Master diplomacy, charm, and subtlety.  
25. **Re-create yourself** — Craft your identity deliberately.  
26. **Keep your hands clean** — Avoid direct involvement in messy actions.  
27. **Play on people’s need to believe** — Create a cult-like following.  
28. **Act boldly** — Boldness creates momentum.  
29. **Plan all the way to the end** — Anticipate obstacles and consequences.  
30. **Make accomplishments seem effortless** — Hide the hard work.


### **31–40: Manipulation, Timing, and Control**
31. **Control the options** — Let others choose, but only from your menu.  
32. **Play to people’s fantasies** — Fantasy is more powerful than reality.  
33. **Discover each person’s weakness** — Everyone has a “handle.”  
34. **Act like royalty** — Carry yourself with confidence and dignity.  
35. **Master the art of timing** — Power depends on choosing the right moment.  
36. **Disdain what you cannot have** — Ignoring reduces its power over you.  
37. **Create compelling spectacles** — Drama captures attention.  
38. **Think as you like, but behave like others** — Blend in to avoid scrutiny.  
39. **Stir waters to catch fish** — Create confusion to gain advantage.  
40. **Despise the free lunch** — What is free often has hidden costs.

### **41–48: Legacy, Adaptation, and Mastery**
41. **Avoid stepping into a great person’s shoes** — Create your own legacy.  
42. **Strike the shepherd to scatter the sheep** — Remove the leader to weaken the group.  
43. **Work on hearts and minds** — Influence emotions, not just logic.  
44. **Disarm with the mirror effect** — Reflect people’s behavior back at them.  
45. **Preach change but never reform too much** — People fear sudden shifts.  
46. **Never appear too perfect** — Perfection breeds envy.  
47. **Do not go past the mark** — Know when to stop.  
48. **Assume formlessness** — Adapt constantly; rigidity is vulnerability.
