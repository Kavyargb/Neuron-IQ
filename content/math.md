@Beginner
The study of numbers. For example, the Pythagorean theorem states that $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

@Intermediate
A more complex example is the formula for the area of a circle, $A = \pi r^2$.

@Advanced
Here is the beautiful Euler's identity:

$$
e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0
$$

And the Schrödinger equation for quantum mechanics:

$$
i\hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial t} \Psi(\mathbf{r},t) = \left[ \frac{-\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2 + V(\mathbf{r},t) \right] \Psi(\mathbf{r},t)
$$
